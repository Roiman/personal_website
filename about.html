About

I've spent the better part of [15 years] working in and around product — leading teams, setting strategy, and helping companies figure out what to build and why. I've worked at [Company] as a product manager, grown into a Head of Product role at [Company], and since [Year] I've been doing this work independently for founders who need experienced product leadership without a full-time hire.

The companies I work best with are past the early chaos but not yet at the stage where everything runs smoothly. They have a product team — maybe a few PMs, designers, engineers — but nobody's really steering. Priorities shift constantly. Stakeholders pull in different directions. The roadmap exists but nobody quite believes in it. That's the situation I find clarifying, not frustrating.

My view on good product work is pretty straightforward: it starts with talking to customers, making hard choices about what not to build, and making sure everyone on the team understands why they're working on what they're working on. I'm not evangelical about any particular process or framework. What matters is that the team has momentum, leadership trusts the product function, and the thing you're shipping actually moves the business forward.

When I'm not working with clients I'm based in [City]. I occasionally write about product and leadership — you can find that on LinkedIn.
